Axiomatic Mechanics: Income Statement Padding via Indirect Labor Siphoning

A professional framework evaluating how overproduction and tactical categorization shifts under GAAP Absorption Costing transfer operating expenses from the Income Statement directly into Inventory Assets.

The Underlying Core Mechanics

Under GAAP (ASC 330), manufacturing operations use Absorption Costing, meaning all manufacturing overhead (MOH)—both variable and fixed—must be capitalized into inventory unit costs rather than expensed immediately as a period cost. Management can exploit this accounting mechanism to temporarily artificially pad net operating income via two levers:

  • Classification Shifting: Re-classifying indirect labor (e.g., product engineers, quality assurance, floor supervisors) out of SG&A operating expenses (period costs) and into Manufacturing Overhead (product costs).
  • Absorption Overproduction Loop: Boosting production volume far beyond market demand. This dilutes fixed costs over a higher denominator of units. Unsold units are held in Finished Goods inventory on the Balance Sheet, dragging their allocated share of fixed indirect salaries alongside them. Consequently, these expenses bypass Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) on the current Income Statement.
Unit Product Cost = Direct Materials + Direct Labor + (Total Manufacturing Overhead / Units Produced)
Current Period COGS = Unit Product Cost × Units Sold
Capitalized Ending Inventory Asset = Unit Product Cost × (Units Produced - Units Sold)
